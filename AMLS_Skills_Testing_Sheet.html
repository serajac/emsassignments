3
Primary Survey (1° Assessment)
A-B-C-D-E / LIFE THREATS
# Performance Criteria ✔ Done N/A Evaluator Notes
3.1 Airway: Opens and assesses airway patency; manages as indicated (positioning, suction, adjunct) ★ CRITICAL
3.2 Breathing: Assesses rate, depth, effort; applies O₂ / ventilatory support as indicated ★ CRITICAL
3.3 Circulation: Assesses pulse (rate, quality); controls major hemorrhage; evaluates skin CTC ★ CRITICAL
3.4 Disability: Assesses GCS (E/V/M) or AVPU; checks pupils (PERRL); blood glucose if indicated
3.5 Expose: Exposes patient appropriately; identifies additional life threats; maintains warmth/dignity
3.6 Identifies and manages all life-threatening conditions before proceeding ★ CRITICAL
3.7 Determines priority for transport; notifies receiving hospital as appropriate
4
History Taking
OPQRST / SAMPLER
# Performance Criteria ✔ Done N/A Evaluator Notes
4.1 OPQRST — Onset, Provocation/Palliation, Quality, Radiation, Severity, Time/Trend
4.2 SAMPLER — Signs/Sx, Allergies, Medications, Pertinent Hx, Last oral intake, Events, Risk factors
4.3 Obtains past medical/surgical history; identifies relevant comorbidities
4.4 Reviews current medications and last dose; assesses compliance and OTC/herbal use
4.5 Obtains collateral history from family, bystanders, medical alert devices, or documentation
4.6 Investigates pertinent negatives relevant to the chief complaint
5
Secondary Survey (2° Physical Exam)
SYSTEMATIC HEAD-TO-TOE / FOCUSED
# Performance Criteria ✔ Done N/A Evaluator Notes
5.1 Vital Signs: BP, HR, RR, SpO₂, EtCO₂, Temperature, Pain scale (0–10); baseline documented
5.2 Head/Neck: Inspects scalp/face; JVD; tracheal position; C-spine tenderness if indicated
5.3 Chest: Inspects for symmetry/retractions/deformity; auscultates anterior/lateral lung fields bilaterally; percusses as needed
5.4 Cardiovascular: Auscultates heart sounds; assesses peripheral pulses and CRT bilaterally; identifies dysrhythmias via ECG
5.5 Abdomen: Inspects; auscultates bowel sounds; palpates all four quadrants for tenderness, rigidity, guarding, masses
5.6 Pelvis/GU: Assesses pelvic stability; identifies bladder distension or incontinence as clinically relevant
5.7 Extremities: Inspects for edema, cyanosis, clubbing, mottling; assesses motor/sensory/circulation distally
5.8 Neuro: GCS documented; stroke screen (BE-FAST/CPSS); cranial nerve screen if indicated
5.9 Diagnostics: 12-lead ECG acquired and interpreted; BGL checked; SpO₂/EtCO₂ waveform assessed as indicated
5.10 IV/IO access obtained; appropriate fluid therapy initiated if indicated
6
Differential Diagnosis Formation
ASSESSMENT-BASED REASONING
# Performance Criteria ✔ Done N/A Evaluator Notes
6.1 Verbalizes a reasonable working diagnosis based on history and physical exam findings ★ CRITICAL
6.2 Identifies at least 2 differential diagnoses consistent with presenting symptoms ★ CRITICAL
6.3 Prioritizes life-threatening differentials; rules out immediately reversible causes
6.4 Treatment plan aligns logically with working diagnosis; anticipates deterioration
6.5 Communicates diagnostic reasoning clearly to partner/evaluator (thinks out loud)

Reassessment / Ongoing Management
RESPONSE TO TX / TRENDING
# Performance Criteria ✔ Done N/A Evaluator Notes
